    • MOTIVATIONS













    What motivates me is fun. The first thing that registers in my mind when I do something is, "do I want to do this?"
    Of course, someties I'm willing to put in the effort to do things that aren't fun. But even so, even if I do that, my mind always wanders to "I could be doing something fun, instead of doing this."






    Usually, the best way to motivate me through something that isn't fun is to make something else I can do at the same time fun. For example, as I'm writing this, I have Discord open. Why? Well, it motivates me. As long as it's there, I can be confident that my friends are right there for me to talk to, if I want.
    Although, to be fair, I don't instigate conversation while I'm working. If someone pings me, I know it's important and that I can take a break and respond to it. After some back and forth banter, my brain has had the break it deserves, and back to work it goes.



    Even so, breaks are only one part of it. If I can, I can make the workload easier by having fun doing it. I'm basically talking to myself here, slinging jokes at myself, being an absolute goofball via this text on the screen. I can make my work that much less of a dredge by doing that.
    This is actually a tactic I employed in science. Of course, lab reports have to be professional, I don't mess around with that. (Usually.) But whenever I get an opportunity to be casual, I go wild. Makes the work less strenuous, even fun, at times.





    When that's not an option, logic takes the wheel. What will doing this do for me? Is it worth the work?
    In schoolwork, the answer is basically always "yes." The exception is extra credit that I don't need, or that I don't do because I feel my existing grade is high enough, I'll be able to maintain it, yadda yadda yadda I don't feel the extra safety net is necessary.
    While this motivates me, it always makes working an absolute dread. So yeah, I don't really prefer this method.
